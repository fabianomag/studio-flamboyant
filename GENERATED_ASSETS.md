# Generated asset provenance

Fourteen complementary architectural visualizations were generated specifically
for Studio Flamboyant on 18 July 2026. They complete the restored seven-image
post-hero gallery for each fictional study without borrowing photographs,
project authorship or visual identity from a real architecture practice.

The images were created with OpenAI image generation. Under the applicable
[OpenAI Terms of Use](https://openai.com/policies/terms-of-use/), as between the
user and OpenAI and to the extent permitted by law, the user owns the output.
Those terms also warn that output may not be unique. This manifest is provenance
evidence, not a warranty that generative output can never resemble other work.

Every prompt explicitly required fictional, unbuilt architecture and excluded
identifiable real buildings, people, text, logos, signage and watermarks. The
only visual inputs were the already licensed images assigned to the same
fictional study.

## Processing contract

- One built-in image-generation call per asset.
- Every source PNG and final WebP was visually inspected.
- Sharp 0.34.5 encoded WebP at quality 84.
- No source was resized, cropped or upscaled.
- Original PNGs remain in Codex local generated-image storage; stable source IDs
  and hashes below allow them to be identified without publishing a machine path.

## Prompt record

All prompts shared this closing constraint:

> Preserve one coherent fictional, unbuilt project with physically plausible
> construction. Do not reproduce an identifiable real building. No people,
> text, logo, signage or watermark. Avoid fantasy geometry, decorative clutter
> and glossy CGI surfaces.

The asset-specific instructions were:

- Horizon 04 — vertical close oblique corner at blue hour: pale exposed
  concrete portal, thin cantilever, floor-to-ceiling glass, still reflecting
  water and restrained coral-amber interior light.
- Horizon 05 — portrait interior from the double-height glazed pavilion toward
  the horizon and reflecting pool; concrete frame on one side, pale stone, low
  sculptural seating and a narrow concealed amber light.
- Horizon 06 — elevated three-quarter 3:2 exterior making the full platform,
  tall portal, thin horizontal roof, linear path and shallow reflecting plane
  legible at the final minutes of daylight.
- Horizon 07 — portrait material detail where board-formed concrete, roof edge,
  frameless glass, pale stone and water meet, lit by cool dusk and one restrained
  amber grazing line.
- Horizon 08 — panoramic lateral nightfall view through a long transparent
  living gallery, with the concrete portal anchoring one end and a continuous
  cobalt/amber water reflection.
- Mist 05 — portrait sheltered threshold in blackened timber and glass beside a
  snow-edged lake, fog and mountains, with only a restrained amber interior.
- Mist 06 — portrait living room with charred-timber ceiling, concrete hearth,
  dark stone, one low wool sofa and full-height glass toward the fog-covered
  winter lake.
- Mist 07 — 3:2 landward arrival through sparse leafless trees: a snow-dusted
  path reveals the flat-roofed concrete and blackened-timber house embedded in
  the slope.
- Mist 08 — low water-level 3:2 closing view at blue hour, layering dark roof,
  illuminated glass, concrete plinth, drifting mist and the lake reflection.
- Courtyard 04 — portrait interior threshold with oak slats, a multi-stem tree,
  pale stone, moss and filtered light beneath a precise roof opening.
- Courtyard 05 — wide exterior of the low U-shaped residence embracing its
  planted central courtyard in late-afternoon light.
- Courtyard 06 — portrait material threshold where a fine-batten oak pivot
  screen meets pale honed limestone and a slim dark-bronze glazed frame.
- Courtyard 07 — long dining-room axis directly connected to the courtyard by
  frameless glazing and oak screens, with pale stone and a linear skylight.
- Courtyard 08 — blue-hour interior/courtyard sequence through glazing and oak
  screens, anchored by the multi-stem tree and discreet 2700K light.
